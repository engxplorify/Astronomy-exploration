Neptune is a representative ice giant environment in the outer Solar System. Its atmosphere is dominated by hydrogen, helium, and methane, with high-speed winds, strong convection, and notable seasonal variability. Observations of Neptune and Triton are crucial for understanding planetary migration, extreme climates, and satellite evolution.
